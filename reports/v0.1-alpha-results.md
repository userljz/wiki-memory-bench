## Oracle / Non-Oracle Explanation

- `non-oracle`: the row does not use gold labels during retrieval or answering. These rows are eligible for fair system-to-system comparisons within the limits of the benchmark slice.
- `oracle`: the row uses gold labels directly at runtime and must be interpreted only as an upper bound, not as a deployable baseline.
- `full-context-oracle` is an oracle upper-bound system and is excluded from fair non-oracle comparisons.
- `gold-evidence-selection`: the row uses gold evidence labels to choose retrieval context. The ClipWiki mode that may do this is `clipwiki --mode oracle-curated`.
- `full-wiki` and `curated` ClipWiki modes are non-oracle modes. They must not read `gold_evidence`, `answer_session_ids`, or `has_answer` labels.
